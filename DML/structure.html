Lab Structure

A project is proposed by a department faculty member for purposes of mathematical research, experiments and visualizations. Once a project is accepted, the lab will propose a team consisting of a faculty mentor, a graduate student mentor and 2-4 undergraduate students. The entire team is expected to meet every 2 weeks. Undergraduate and graduate members are expected to meet more often, with undergraduate members contributing 3-4 hours per week to the project. At the end of the quarter, each team will present a poster at the open house.

Faculty mentor

Faculty mentors are responsible for regular meetings with the team as they work on the proposed project.

Graduate mentor

Graduate members are resposible for supervising progress on the project and proving both mathematical and technical support to the undergraduate student members.

Graduate mentor

Undergraduate students work in small groups under the mentorship of a graduate student and a faculty member on a research project and present their discovery to the department members. Students are required to register for MAT 199 and commit at least 3 hours per week to their project.
